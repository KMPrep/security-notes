PPTP — Point-to-Point Tunneling Protocol

OSI Layer: 5 — Session (tunnels Layer 2 PPP frames)
RFC: RFC 2637

Introduction

PPTP is a tunneling protocol developed by Microsoft and partners to create VPN connections by encapsulating PPP frames inside IP packets using GRE (Generic Routing Encapsulation). It was widely used in the 1990s and early 2000s for dial-up and corporate VPNs due to its simplicity and built-in Windows support. PPTP is now considered cryptographically broken and insecure — its MS-CHAPv2 authentication and MPPE encryption have known vulnerabilities — and has been replaced by IPsec, L2TP/IPsec, and WireGuard.
