Microsoft Purview — Compliance & DLP

Category: Microsoft 365 Data Governance / Compliance & Information Protection
Key standards / frameworks: Microsoft Purview, NIST 800-53 (AC-4, AU-2, SI-4), ISO 27001 A.5.12/A.8.12, GDPR, HIPAA, SEC 17a-4, MITRE ATT&CK T1530 / T1114

Overview

Microsoft Purview is the compliance and data governance suite of Microsoft 365. Where Entra ID governs identity and Defender governs threats, Purview governs data: classifying it, protecting it, retaining or deleting it, investigating it, and proving compliance. Its capabilities span the entire M365 estate because they operate on the shared substrate that Exchange, SharePoint, OneDrive, and Teams all use.

The suite is best understood as layers that build on one another. Classification through sensitivity labels feeds protection through encryption and DLP. Lifecycle through retention labels and records management controls how long data persists. Investigation through eDiscovery, the Unified Audit Log, Communication Compliance, and Insider Risk Management provides the evidentiary and behavioral backbone. Most advanced capabilities require Microsoft 365 E5 or targeted compliance add-on licensing, which is a real planning constraint.

How It Works

Classification and Protection

Sensitivity labels are the core of Microsoft Information Protection. A label can be applied manually, recommended, or auto-applied based on sensitive information types and trainable classifiers. When a label carries encryption, it invokes rights management so the file is encrypted and usage rights such as view, edit, and print are bound to identities; protection then travels with the document even outside the tenant. Labels apply to files, emails, and container objects like sites and teams.

Data Loss Prevention

DLP policies detect sensitive content and enforce actions across multiple locations in one policy: Exchange email, SharePoint, OneDrive, Teams chat and channel messages, and managed endpoints through Endpoint DLP. Actions range from user notification and policy tips to blocking sharing, blocking egress to USB or cloud upload, and generating alerts. DLP can match on built-in sensitive information types, exact data match, document fingerprints, or a sensitivity label.

Lifecycle and Investigation

Retention labels and policies keep content for a required period and then delete it, or preserve it immutably for records management with regulatory or event-based retention. eDiscovery comes in Standard and Premium tiers, where Premium adds legal hold, custodian management, advanced review, and analytics. Insider Risk Management scores risky user behavior into cases, Communication Compliance scans messages for policy violations, and the Unified Audit Log records tenant activity as the common evidence source.

Detection & Telemetry

The Unified Audit Log is the backbone of investigation, recording operations across all workloads with events like DLPRuleMatch, SensitivityLabelApplied, Set-RetentionCompliancePolicy, and eDiscovery search activity. Purview surfaces DLP and Insider Risk alerts in the compliance portal and forwards them to Defender XDR for correlation, while Communication Compliance flags policy-violating messages. Entra ID logs record who accessed compliance tooling, and Audit Premium plus SIEM export extend retention and enable long-horizon threat hunting.

Key Takeaways

  • Purview governs the data plane: classify, protect, retain, investigate, and prove compliance across Exchange, SharePoint, OneDrive, and Teams.
  • Sensitivity labels with encryption make protection travel with the file, remaining enforced even outside the tenant.
  • One DLP policy can span email, SharePoint, OneDrive, Teams, and endpoints; coverage gaps and test mode are the common failure, not the engine.
  • Roll DLP out from audit to block with policy tips to avoid over-blocking that pushes users to bypass controls.
  • Records management with preservation lock provides immutable, regulator-grade retention that ordinary retention cannot.
  • The Unified Audit Log is the shared evidence source; enabling it and extending its retention is foundational to every investigation.
  • Most advanced Purview capabilities require E5 or compliance add-ons, so validate licensing before assuming a control is active.
